Public Speaking for Sales

Use CTRL+F to search into the page

 1)  Jolene is applying for a sales position. The interviewer explains that it will be an inside sales position selling office supplies. Which of the following will be true of Jolene’s job, if she gets it, based on William Cook’s characterization of inside sales jobs? Select one:

  She will be driving a lot.
  She will have to go out to meet clients.
  The client will not have an appointment when meeting her.
  She will be paid on commission only.

 2)  Nick is applying for a sales position. The interviewer explains that it will be an outside sales position selling office supplies. Which of the following will be true of Nick’s job, if he gets it, based on William Cook’s characterization of inside and outside sales jobs? Select one:

  Clients will usually come to the store where Nick works.
  He will be driving a lot.
  He will work with an inflexible, set schedule.
  He will get paid an hourly wage.

 3)  Joan sells microwaves to hotels for a living. She has to determine the lifetime value of her clients, since she expects to stay on the job for 20 years. The microwaves cost $200 each, and each unit has a 20% profit margin. Joan anticipates selling 300 units to Holiday Inn over the rest of her career. According to “The Power of Selling” what is the anticipated lifetime value of Holiday Inn? Select one:

  $900
  $12,000
  $18,000
  $20,000

 4)  Brenda is getting ready to start searching for sales positions. She describes herself as a self-motivated free spirit who works best when she is not confined by a schedule or even physical surroundings. She craves change and spontaneity. Which type of sales position should Brenda seek? Select one:

  Inside sales position
  Outside sales position
  Neither
  Either

 5)  “Will delivery on Tuesday work for you?” “Will 2,000 copies be enough?” “Should I schedule it in to start on Friday?” These are all standard ways to: Select one:

  qualify the prospect.
  ask for the order.
  transition to selling.
  enhance your ethos.

 6)  Phyllis was having trouble closing sales. After consulting with more experienced sales people at her company, she finally realized the surprising reason for her failures. She was not closing sales because she was failing at the initial stage of the close, which is: Select one:

  asking for the sale.
  quoting a price.
  qualifying prospects.
  presenting the product/service.

 7)  Jackson has learned that in advertising sales, the sales are often made after a few meetings, not just one. He tries hard to focus on a customer-centric approach to meeting his clients’ advertising needs while delivering value to the customer. Which communication skill, according to “The Presentation: The Power of Solving Problems,” does Jackson have to rely on more? Select one:

  Speaking
  Nonverbal communication
  Kinesics
  Listening

 8)  Joe is a sales manager who has to hire a new sales person. He needs to recruit candidates on a limited budget. He has been told that the owners of the company value growth, but at this time there are limited resources to use to find the best job candidate. Which type of recruitment should Joe use? Select one:

  Recycled
  Internet
  Internal
  External

 9)  Toni is a very successful sales professional. Clients report that they have great trust in Toni and enjoy the relationship they have established. Other clients report that the things Toni says to them are pretty close to what they are already thinking. Which of the following characteristics from “The Power of Selling” does Toni excel in? Select one:

  The ability to connect
  The willingness to take risks
  The willingness to learn
  The drive to succeed

 10)  Rakesh is in Chicago for the first time ever. He needs to buy a phone charger since he left his back home in California. He goes in the first store he finds because he just wants to buy the charger and move on. He knows he will never return to the store or likely ever go to Chicago again. Which type of sales relationship did Rakesh engage in? Select one:

  Functional relationship
  Affiliative relationship
  Strategic partnership
  Transactional relationship

 11)  Fill in the blank. The sales profession differs from most other career paths in various ways. One difference is how sales people are paid. For example, Raul makes much more money than Betty even though they both work in sales for the same company and were hired at the same time. Raul, however, sells twice as much as Betty and, therefore, makes twice as much money. This pay disparity is possible because sales is a __________ profession. Select one:

  randomized success
  gender biased
  pay-for-performance
  fixed income

 12)  Theresa has to buy office supplies. She wants to deal with a sales person who will be willing to meet at 10 a.m. at the sales store, as that is the only time she will usually have available. She has an option of going with an inside or outside sales person. Which should Theresa choose? Select one:

  Inside sales person
  Outside sales person
  It does not matter.
  She should consult the manager.

 13)  According to Bill Caskey and Bryan Neale, how can you, as a sales person, best demonstrate value to a customer in a way that the customer will trust you? Select one:

  Tell the customer why you are trustworthy
  Allow someone else to tell the customer why you are trustworthy
  Let your actions show the customer how you are trustworthy
  Don’t tell the customer anything

 14)  Tina sells sea shells, and she says this to a prospective buyer: “Justin, I can get you 500 shells by this Friday. Would you like them to be all small, large, or mixed sizes?” Which type of close did Tina use? Select one:

  Direct request
  Benefit summary
  Assumptive
  Alternative choice

 15)  Akhil is selling copiers to organizations. He is going to a sales call. He stops to change his shirt to a plain white, button down collar as that is what most of the people at the next sales call will be wearing. He also deletes the two funny pictures from his power point presentation because the people he will be speaking to are very formal. Which of the 4 P’s of Presentation Preparation is Akhil following? Select one:

  Prioritize the agenda
  Personalize
  Prepare illustrations
  Practice

 16)  Jane works in sales for ACME Brick Company. She lives in Tennessee and is responsible for working with construction firms operating in Nashville and Knoxville Tennessee. This helps Jane focus her prospecting and contact efforts. These areas of responsibility are Jane’s: Select one:

  audience analysis.
  sales territory.
  compatriot business partners.

  managers.
  owners.
  customers.

 23)  Sally sells crop duster airplanes. Her clients do not make purchases often, but when they do they are significant sales. A lot of time is spent on the buying decision, and the clients rely heavily on Sally’s cutting-edge knowledge of the airplane industry and regulations. Clients spend hundreds of thousands of dollars on these purchases, so they must be able to trust what Sally is telling them. Which type of sales relationship are Sally and her clients engaged in? Select one:

 24)  From Laith Alrubaiee’s study on ethics of sales behavior among bankers, which of the following would be the most accurate summary regarding how bank financial advisors should be trained? Select one:

  Ethics should be a part of sales training.
  Ethics should be a step in the sales presentation process.
  Ethics should not be a part of training because people will either be, or not be, ethical.
  Ethics should be taught before selling because ethics are more important.

Roger had previously attended a seminar about the oven he is selling where he was told that the manufacturer conducted rigorous tests on the model 315 and that it performed better than any other on the market. In talking with the customer, Roger said he conducted the tests himself and that the 315 was the best on the market. What did Roger do? Select one:

  Hyperbolize
  Close the sale
  Plagiarize
  Gain attention
