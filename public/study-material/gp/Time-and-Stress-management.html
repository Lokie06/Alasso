Public Speaking for Sales

Use CTRL+F to search into the page

 1) This morning you woke up to twice as many new e-mails in your inbox as normal. What should you do? Select one:
  Respond to the oldest e-mail
  Respond to the newest e-mail
  Respond to the most important e-mail
  Go through and delete all of the irrelevant e-mails

 2) Doing which of the following tasks at night, rather than in the morning, will help you reduce unproductive time at the beginning of your day? Select one:

  Exercise
  Watch TV
  Lay out your outfit
  Work until midnight

 3) What is one of the easiest ways to save time during your day? Select one:

  Exercise instead of eating lunch
  Pack your lunch instead of buying it
  Meditate instead of using social media
  Create a routine instead of approaching the day without a plan

 4) What is one reason people struggle with managing their time effectively? Select one:

  They never procrastinate.
  They give in to distractions.
  They track their time too closely.
  They know what stimuli triggers their creativity.

 5) This semester, you are taking courses totaling 10 credit hours. How much time should you devote to studying each week, at minimum? Select one:

  10 hours
  15 hours
  20 hours
  40 hours

 6) Which of the following statements about multi-tasking is true? Select one:

  Our brains are good at it.
  It is essential to getting things done.
  It is an effective time-management method.
  We do not have the ability to truly multi-task.

 7) What might be a reason for your procrastination? Select one:

  You are lazy.
  You are too organized.
  You feel overwhelmed.
  You have overdeveloped decision-making skills.

 8) What is the easiest way to identify what distracts you? Select one:

  Log your time
  Read about distractions online
  Ask others about their distractions
  Think about distractions during meditation

 9) Fill in the blank. Successful goals have been called S.M.A.R.T. This is an acronym for smart, _______________, attainable, realistic, and _______________. Select one:

  manageable; timely
  measurable; timely
  manageable; task-oriented
  measurable; task-oriented

 10) You tend to shut down when you are under stress. What type of stress-relieving activity will be most helpful to you? Select one:

  Go for a short walk
  Hold a comforting object
  Look at funny pictures on your phone
  Sip a steaming cup of coffee or tea, or have a refreshing cold drink

 11) Effective stress management puts you in charge of your thoughts, emotions, schedule, and the way you deal with problems. What is the first step to begin to manage your stress? Select one:

  Alter the situation
  Adapt to the stressor
  Avoid unnecessary stress
  Identify the sources of stress

 12) _______________ can be used to record your thoughts or feelings at regular intervals throughout the day (i.e. every hour) so that you have a comprehensive record of the things that cause you stress. Select one:

  A daily log
  A stress diary
  Voice narration
  A calendar planner

 13) You have 24 hours to sleep, eat, work, and do all your personal tasks. Which is the most effective way to do more in your day? Select one:

  Eat less
  Sleep less
  Work more
  Take more breaks

 14) Which of the following strategies helps you make the most of your limited time? Select one:

  Prioritize your tasks
  Take a 20-minute break every hour
  Do as much as you can before lunch
  Picture your schedule mentally throughout the day

 15) What is one reason people struggle with managing their time effectively? Select one:

  They never procrastinate.
  They give in to distractions.
  They track their time too closely.
  They know what stimuli triggers their creativity.

 16) Which of the following is a common physiological response to stress? Select one:

  Sweat
  Muscle fatigue
  Tear production
  Increased heart rate

 17) When you replace a high-priority activity with a low priority activity, you are: Select one:

  relaxing.
  organizing.
  scheduling.
  procrastinating.

 18) Which of the following is a good prioritization tool? Select one:

  The ABC technique
  The A-Zs of priority
  Cognitive restructuring
  Evaluate the cost of your time

 19) What is a general tip that will help you stop procrastinating? Select one:

  Ask others for help
  Make up a reward system for yourself
  Physically separate yourself from others
  Identify the pleasant consequences of not doing the task

 20) Johnny just received a failing grade on his science exam. Lucy oversaw Johnny’s paper, and she asked him about it. Johnny snapped at her shouting, “It’s none of your business!” Which of the following is the psychological response Johnny exhibited towards Lucy? Select one:

  Dismay
  Irritability
  Hesitation
  Hopelessness

 21) What stress management strategy are you using when you pare down a to-do list? Select one:

  Alter the situation
  Adapt to the stressor
  Avoid unnecessary stress
  Accept things you cannot change

 22) As it relates to stress, what is resilience? Select one:

  An ability to adapt to tasks
  Using your heart for emotion
  To maintain a firmness of character
  Learning how to keep one’s anger to oneself

 23) Many practical stress-management techniques can help individuals with reducing stress, increase individuals’ feeling in control of their life, and promote overall: Select one:

  balance.
  well-being.
  mental health.
  physical health.

 24) What stress management strategy are you employing when you try to view a stressful situation from a more positive perspective? Select one:

  Altering
  Adapting
  Avoiding
  Accepting

 25) Greg just got back from lunch, when his manager came in to give him bad news: Starting this afternoon, Greg is going to have to work an extra 20 hours this week to get a project finished. Greg is stressed. Which of the following is a productive stress coping mechanism for Greg at that moment? Select one:

  Take a nap
  Take a drive
  Squeeze a stress ball
  Buy a new pair of jeans
