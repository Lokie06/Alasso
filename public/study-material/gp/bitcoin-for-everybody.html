 1) Which of the following best describes money?

  A shared belief held by the members of society
  A battery for society, like stored energy to be drawn on by users
  A good selected for by society based on objective characteristics

 2) Why did the classical gold standard break down?

  Attacks by speculative investors caused price fluctuations
  Governments no longer kept the monetary promises they were entrusted with
  Paper currencies provided more productivity by enabling the faster flow of money

 3) Why is durability important for money?

  Being durable is a show of "unforgeable costliness"
  Being hard to destroy helps make it a more impressive monetary good
  If it were easily destroyed, people could no longer trade using that monetary good

 4) Who benefits from fiat inflation, and what are the consequences of it?

 People with money in a savings account benefit since they will receive increased interest rates
 People who are owed money benefit since it becomes easier for the person in debt to repay the debt
 Debt holders benefit since their debts are 'inflated away', which creates incentives to take on unsustainable debts

 5) What does proof of work in Bitcoin achieve?

 It reduces the computational load on the computers participating in the network
 It ties the physical world to consensus in Bitcoin's ledger since it is costly to produce
 It helps identify prime numbers that may have applications in other areas of science and technology

 6) Over recent decades, what has happened to the "risk-free rate of return"?

 It has gone up

 7) If Bitcoin were to succeed at becoming widely used as money, why would this be?

 8) Why is it unlikely that governments in the free world will ban Bitcoin?

  Bitcoin operates completely outside the purview of all governments in the world, and is therefore "unbannable"

 9) What were the "crypto wars" about?

  Disagreements between the intelligence community and privacy activists over the rights of citizens to use end-to-end encryption

 10) What was a distinguishing feature of Nick Szabo's Bit Gold?

 12) What would happen if the Bitcoin blockchain had multiple competing branches?

  Potential 'ties' would be broken as new blocks are formed, since the network switches to the longest branch available
  A developer would have to intervene to select which chain is correct, which is why developers need to be supported
